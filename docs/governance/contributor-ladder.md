# Contributor Ladder

The path from first-time contributor to maintainer. This formalizes the
ladder introduced in the [community model](community-model.md). Progression
is based on **demonstrated, sustained contribution** — there is no fixed
tenure requirement, and every rung is open to contributors from across
government, academia, and the wider community.

## Rungs

### 1. Contributor

Anyone who opens a PR, issue, RFC, or translation. No special access
required. First contributions are welcomed and mentored.

**How to start:** read [CONTRIBUTING.md](../../CONTRIBUTING.md), pick an
issue (or a translation), open a PR.

### 2. Regular Contributor

Sustained, quality contributions over time. May be invited to help triage
issues and review PRs in areas they're active in.

**Recognition:** proposed by a maintainer based on a track record.

### 3. Reviewer (domain)

A Regular Contributor with domain expertise — **accessibility**,
**localization** (per language), **security**, or a specific package —
granted **required sign-off** authority in that domain
([accessibility](accessibility-review-process.md) /
[localization](localization-review-process.md) reviews).

**Expectations:** apply the relevant review process consistently and
fairly; keep the domain's glossary/standards current.

### 4. Package Maintainer

Merge rights on one or more `packages/*` workspaces; owns that package's
quality, roadmap, and releases
([maintainer responsibilities](maintainer-responsibilities.md)).

### 5. Lead Maintainer

Overall direction and final authority; steward of sustainability.

## How progression works

- A contributor is **nominated by an existing maintainer** for the next
  rung, based on evidence (merged work, reviews, RFCs, mentoring).
- Maintainers reach consensus (lead breaks ties) per the
  [governance model](governance-model.md).
- The change is recorded openly (a MAINTAINERS/OWNERS file as the team
  grows).

## Moving down / emeritus

Stepping back is normal and no-fault. Inactive reviewers/maintainers move
to **emeritus** to keep the active set accurate; they're welcome back when
they have time again.

## Recognition beyond code

Translation, accessibility testing, documentation, triage, and community
support are **first-class contributions** and count toward progression —
not just code.
