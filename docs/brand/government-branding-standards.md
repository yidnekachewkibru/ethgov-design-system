# Government Branding Standards

ETHDS is used across the whole Ethiopian government estate — national
ministries and agencies, regional governments, and municipal/woreda
administrations, as well as public institutions. These standards keep
those services recognisably part of one government while still allowing
each body to be clearly identified.

The goal: a citizen can tell at a glance that a service is **official
Ethiopian government**, and can tell **which body** delivers it — in that
order of priority. This operationalises
[Consistent Government Experience](../design-principles/09-consistent-government-experience.md).

## The Shared Identity Comes First

- The shared government identity — the [colour system](color.md),
  [typography](typography.md), and common
  [header/footer](../phases/phase-7-core-components.md) — is the constant
  across every service.
- **Ethiopian Star Blue (`blue-600`)** is the shared primary brand colour
  for all bodies. Individual ministries do not substitute their own
  primary interaction colour; consistency of the interaction colour is
  part of what makes government services feel like one government.
- The delivering body is identified **within** this shared identity (name,
  approved logo per [logo-usage.md](logo-usage.md)), never by replacing
  the shared visual language.

## Levels of Government

| Level | Identity treatment |
|---|---|
| **National (ministries, agencies)** | National emblem + ministry/agency name in the standard header; shared palette and type. |
| **Regional governments** | National identity present; regional government named and (where applicable) its approved regional emblem shown as the delivering body, within the shared system. |
| **Municipal / woreda** | Same pattern: shared identity first, municipality/woreda named as the delivering body. |
| **Public institutions (e.g. universities, exam bodies)** | Shared identity signals official status; the institution is identified as the delivering body. |

In every case the structure is the same — **shared government identity +
delivering body** — so citizens learn one pattern and apply it everywhere.

## What Bodies May Customise

- **May:** their name and approved logo/emblem as the delivering body;
  their specific content, services, and supported regional language
  emphasis (while still meeting
  [Multilingual by Default](../design-principles/02-multilingual-by-default.md)).
- **Must not:** change the primary interaction colour, swap the type
  system, alter the header/footer structure, restyle shared components, or
  introduce a separate visual identity that competes with the shared one.

Genuine needs for variation are raised through an
[RFC](../../CONTRIBUTING.md#proposing-significant-changes) so any change
benefits the whole system rather than fragmenting it
([Reuse Before Building](../design-principles/07-reuse-before-building.md)).

## Language & Naming

- Service and body names appear in the citizen's
  [active language](../design-principles/02-multilingual-by-default.md);
  the identity must work across both Ge'ez and Latin scripts (see
  [typography.md](typography.md)).
- Name services by the citizen's task, not the internal org name
  ([Start with Citizen Needs](../design-principles/01-start-with-citizen-needs.md)),
  with the delivering body shown as supporting identification.

## Trust & Anti-Impersonation

A consistent official identity helps citizens distinguish genuine
services from fraudulent imitations — especially important for services
handling identity, payments, and immigration. Bodies must not dilute the
shared identity in ways that make official services harder to recognise,
and should use official government domains and the standard identity so
citizens have reliable signals of authenticity.
