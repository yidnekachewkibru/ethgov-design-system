# Community Model

## Contributor Ladder

1. **Contributor** — anyone who opens a PR, issue, or RFC. No special
   access required.
2. **Regular Contributor** — sustained, quality contributions over time.
   May be invited to review PRs in their area of activity.
3. **Reviewer** — a Regular Contributor with domain expertise (e.g.
   accessibility, localization, a specific package) granted sign-off
   authority in that domain, per the [governance model](governance-model.md).
4. **Package Maintainer** — merge rights on one or more `packages/*`
   workspaces.
5. **Lead Maintainer** — overall project direction and final authority.

Progression up the ladder is based on demonstrated, sustained
contribution and is proposed by an existing maintainer — there is no
fixed tenure requirement. See the full
[contributor ladder](contributor-ladder.md) for how each step works in
practice.

## Communication Channels

- **GitHub Issues** — bugs and feature requests
- **GitHub Discussions** — open-ended questions and proposals before they
  become a formal RFC
- **Pull Requests** — code and documentation review
- **RFCs**
  ([`packages/ethds-rfcs/`](https://github.com/yidnekachewkibru/ethgov-design-system/tree/main/packages/ethds-rfcs))
  — significant design and architecture decisions

A dedicated public communication channel (chat/forum) will be evaluated
once the contributor base grows past what GitHub-native tools comfortably
support.

## Code of Conduct Enforcement

All channels are covered by
[`CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md`](https://github.com/yidnekachewkibru/ethgov-design-system/blob/main/CODE_OF_CONDUCT.md).
Reports are handled by maintainers per the enforcement guidelines in that
document. The designated conduct-report contact will be published here
once the maintainer team is established beyond the project founder.

## Inclusivity

Given ETHDS serves citizens across Ethiopia's linguistic and regional
diversity, the contributor community is explicitly intended to include
participants from outside the national government — regional and
municipal government technologists, academic institutions, and
individual open-source contributors are all first-class participants,
not just consumers of work done elsewhere.
